AI

Contributors: Jonathan Miu, someone, someone, someone

scroll down

It is a very real possibility that, one day, robots and artificial intelligence will make humans obsolete. In fact, for many jobs that fleshy organisms once held, like pulling carriages and assembly lines, are now performed by robots. We once believed that robots could only handle simple, repetitive tasks, those that can be defined with math. But the truth is, robots have us beat in many situations already. Self-driving cars and trucks will soon take many transportation jobs, screens and self-checkouts take more service jobs every year, and even music is being written by robots now.
